The beginning

My Story

I started Backpacker Journal in 2019 after quitting my corporate job to travel the world. What began as a 6-month sabbatical turned into a lifestyle that has taken me to over 30 countries across 6 continents.

My first solo trip was to Southeast Asia, where I discovered that travel doesn't have to be expensive to be life-changing. I learned to navigate foreign cities, communicate across language barriers, and find authentic experiences that most tourists miss.

Since then, I've lived out of a 40L backpack, working remotely as a freelance writer while exploring the world. From teaching English in Vietnam to volunteering in Nepal, each experience has shaped my perspective on travel and life.

This blog is my way of sharing the lessons I've learned, the people I've met, and the places that have changed me. I believe that travel is the best education, and I want to help others discover the world on their own terms.

How I travel

My Travel Philosophy

Four principles that guide every journey and every trip I curate.

Travel Slow

I believe in spending quality time in each destination rather than rushing through a checklist. This allows for deeper connections with locals and a more authentic understanding of the culture.

Travel Light

Carrying less means more freedom and flexibility. My 7kg packing list has everything I need for indefinite travel, proving that less really is more.

Travel Local

I seek out local experiences, street food, and off-the-beaten-path destinations. The best travel memories come from unexpected encounters and authentic moments.

Travel Responsibly

I'm committed to sustainable travel practices, supporting local businesses, and leaving a positive impact on the communities I visit.
