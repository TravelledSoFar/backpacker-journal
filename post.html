Vietnam on a Budget:
2-Week Itinerary

How to explore Vietnam's highlights for under $25/day — from Hanoi's bustling Old Quarter to Ho Chi Minh City's vibrant streets.

Pooja

Travel Writer

Vietnam is one of the most budget-friendly destinations in Southeast Asia, offering incredible value for money without sacrificing experiences. In this guide, I'll share my complete 2-week itinerary that covers the country's highlights while keeping costs under $25 per day.

Quick Facts

  • Duration: 14 days
  • Budget: $350 total ($25/day)
  • Route: Hanoi → Halong Bay → Hoi An → Ho Chi Minh City
  • Best Time: February to April (dry season)

Day 1–3: Hanoi

Start your journey in Vietnam's charming capital. Hanoi offers a perfect introduction to Vietnamese culture with its bustling Old Quarter, delicious street food, and rich history.

Where to Stay

Hanoi Backpackers Hostel — $8/night for a dorm bed, located in the heart of the Old Quarter. Clean, social, and includes breakfast.

Must-Do Activities

  • Explore the Old Quarter on foot (free)
  • Visit Hoan Kiem Lake and Ngoc Son Temple ($2)
  • Take a street food tour ($15)
  • Visit the Vietnam Museum of Ethnology ($2)

Food Budget

Street food is incredibly cheap and delicious in Hanoi:

  • Pho (noodle soup): $1–2
  • Banh mi (Vietnamese sandwich): $1
  • Fresh spring rolls: $2
  • Vietnamese coffee: $1

Day 4–5: Halong Bay

A UNESCO World Heritage site, Halong Bay is famous for its emerald waters and thousands of limestone islands. While it can be expensive, there are budget options available.

Budget Cruise Option

Halong Bay Budget Cruise — $45 for a 2-day, 1-night cruise including meals, kayaking, and cave visits. Book through your hostel for the best deals.

Day 6–9: Hoi An

This charming ancient town is known for its lantern-lit streets, excellent food, and affordable tailoring. It's also a great place to slow down and soak in the atmosphere.

Where to Stay

Hoi An Backpackers — $6/night for a dorm bed, located just outside the Old Town (saving on accommodation costs).

Activities

  • Explore the Old Town (free during the day, $6 for evening ticket)
  • Take a cooking class ($20)
  • Visit the beach (free)
  • Get clothes tailored (prices vary, but very affordable)

Day 10–14: Ho Chi Minh City

End your journey in Vietnam's largest city, where you'll find a mix of history, culture, and modern development.

Where to Stay

Saigon Backpackers — $7/night for a dorm bed in District 1, close to all major attractions.

Must-See Attractions

  • Cu Chi Tunnels ($15 including transport)
  • War Remnants Museum ($2)
  • Notre-Dame Cathedral (free)
  • Ben Thanh Market (free to explore)

Transportation

Vietnam has an excellent and affordable transportation network:

  • Overnight buses: $15–25 between cities
  • Local buses: $0.50–1 per trip
  • Motorbike taxis: $1–3 for short trips
  • Grab (ride-sharing): Similar to Uber, very affordable

Budget Breakdown

14-Day Budget Breakdown

Accommodation (13 nights) $91
Food ($8/day) $112
Transportation $60
Activities & Attractions $50
Miscellaneous $37
Total $350

Money-Saving Tips

  • Eat street food: It's not only cheaper but often more authentic than restaurants
  • Stay in dorms: Hostels offer great value and social opportunities
  • Book tours through hostels: They often have better rates than online booking sites
  • Use local transport: Avoid tourist taxis when possible
  • Bargain at markets: It's expected and can save you significant money

Final Thoughts

Vietnam offers incredible value for budget travelers. With careful planning and these tips, you can experience the country's highlights without breaking the bank. The key is to embrace local experiences, eat where the locals eat, and be open to new adventures.
